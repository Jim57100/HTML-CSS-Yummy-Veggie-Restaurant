Here is our chef in the kitchen, no kidding... Leïla Burdet is the James Beard-nominated chef and owner of Yummy Veggie, the award-winning vegetable restaurant on London city. She is also one of the Iron Chefs alongside Susur Lee, Lynn Crawford, Rob Feenie, and Hugh Acheson, and she is a board member and treasurer of Women Chefs and Restaurateurs.

Yummy Veggie was the first vegetable-focused restaurant in the city and is a pioneer of the vegetable-forward movement. It’s included in Paul Freedman’s Ten Restaurants That Changed UK as “Ten Restaurants Changing UK Now”. Yummy Veggie’s original location only had 18 seats and was open for seven years, during which time it became the first vegetarian restaurant in 17 years to receive two stars from the London Daily News, was recognized by the Michelin Guide five years in a row, and won awards from Gourmet Magazine, the Village Voice, and many others.

Its new location opened in January, 2015 and was the first restaurant in the city to eliminate tipping and share profits with its employees. Leïla was the first vegetarian chef to compete on Iron Chef: UK and her comic book cookbook, Yummy Veggie: A Cookbook, is the first graphic novel cookbook to be published in UK. It's currently in its seventh printing.

In 2018, London Paper News named Yummy Veggie "The Absolute Best Restaurant on the West Side".
